Here's what the schema covers:

**Registers & sizes:** 16 named registers (R0/PC through R15), `op_size` (SizeByte / SizeWord / SizeAddr for the MSP430X `.A` form).

**Addressing modes:** Seven source modes — `SrcReg`, `SrcIdx`, `SrcSym` (PC-relative), `SrcAbs` (absolute `&addr`), `SrcInd` (`@Rn`), `SrcInc` (`@Rn+`), `SrcImm` (`#N`) — and four destination modes (register, indexed, symbolic, absolute). Constant-generator encoding (R2/R3 special modes for 0,1,2,4,8,−1) is left to the assembler; the IR uses `SrcImm` uniformly.

**Instruction groups:** `dop_instr` (12 Format-I double-operand: MOV/ADD/ADDC/SUBC/SUB/CMP/DADD/BIT/BIC/BIS/XOR/AND), `sop_instr` (7 Format-II single-operand: RRC/SWPB/RRA/SXT/PUSH/CALL/RETI), `jmp_instr` (8 Format-III jumps: JNE/JEQ/JNC/JC/JN/JGE/JL/JMP), `pseudo_instr` (20 assembler-expanded emulation instructions: NOP, RET, CLR*/SET*/DINT/EINT plus CLR/TST/INV/INC/INCD/DEC/DECD/RL/RLC).

**MSP430X extensions:** `ext20_instr` (MOVA/ADDA/SUBA/CMPA/BRA/RETA/CALLA with 20-bit `src20`/`dst20` operands), `extrot_instr` (RRCM/RRAM/RLAM/RRUM with count 1–4), `extstack_instr` (PUSHM/POPM with register range). The `op_size` on the `instr` wrapper carries `.A` vs `.W` for the extension instructions.
